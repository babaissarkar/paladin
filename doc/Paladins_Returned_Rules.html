Rules

 

1. General Rules

  1. a. Card limit ― exactly 45 cards per library (standard game).

    b. Copies of cards ―

    1. 1.2 copies of cards with total energy less than 6. 

    2. 2.1 copy of card with cost more than or equal to 6. 

    3. 3.1 additional copy of a card is allowed if that card has type Trap or has ability Trigger.

3. Winning, Losing and Inflicting damage

 A player only wins the game when if his/her opponent's barrier points are reduced to zero or a player has no cards to draw from the library during a draw phase.

 When an opponent's character attacks an opponent unguarded while the opponent has no guard by General, the opponent's barrier point is reduced by the attacking character's or sorcery's damage point.

 When damage is being inflicted, the opponent immediately commences a special phase, known as damage phase.

4. Gameplay

a. beginning game phase :

  1. First, who takes the first turn is decided by a random method, like tossing a coin or rolling a dice. A player may also allow the opponent to start the game.

Then each player takes six cards from the top of their library. These six cards are called the opening collection. If a player does not likes his/her collection, he/she may shuffle those cards back to his/her deck and again take five cards again. This is called Collection refreshing. It can only be done at the beginning of the game and not more than twice.

This phase is done only once, at the start of the game.

b. draw phase :

In this phase, the current player (the player whose turn it is) adds a card to his/her collection. This is called drawing a card.

  1. c.energy phase : 

    In this phase, the player puts a card to the energy zone (known as energising) and disengages all cards in his/her energy zone. This phase is optional. A player can put only one card in the  energy zone per turn. 

    d. summoning phase : 

    I. Sub-phase 1 : using cards for energy 

 The current player engages (turns anticlockwise in a right angle) the required no. of cards in his/her energy zone.

 The player can engage as many cards as he/she wants to, the only limit being the total number of cards in the energy zone and/or card effects. The total energy no. of cards thus engaged represents the currently active energy of that player, which is used for putting cards into the battlefield.

 

II. Sub-phase 2 : putting cards into the battlefield

        The current player may now put any number of cards into the battlefield or materialize them into their specific zones. The only constraint being the active energy. The active energy decreases by the materialized card's total energy whenever a card is materialized. If a General is put into the battlefield, it should be put into the battlefield first. For characters, this process is called summoning and for sorceries, casting.

 

III. Sub-phase 3 : attacking and guarding

 Sub-phases :

 A. Pre-battle phase,

 B. Attack phase,

 C. Post-battle phase.

A. Pre-battle phase :

      1. I. The turn player selects which characters will attack this turn and moves them to the attack zone. This moving of cards from rest zone to attack zone is called engaging. Its reverse procedure is called disengaging.

  1. II.The turn player activates any effects that occur when a character attacks, from left to right. 

    B. Attack phase : 

  2. III.The turn player selects attack targets for those engaged characters. 

  3. IV.Battles occur between characters, if any. 

    C. Post-battle phase : 

  4. V.The total barrier damage is dealt to the opponent, if any and only if the source character which dealt the damage is present in the battle zone during this phase. This includes the damage from Attacker sorceries. 

e. end phase

        The current turn player activates any abilities and effects that occur during the end of the turn. Then, the current player end his/her turn and the opponent plays his/her turn by taking phases b to e. The games continues until the criteria as stated in rule 3 is met.

f. *damage phase

 This is a special phase that is commenced when a player receives damage from the opponent. In this phase, the player puts cards from the top of the deck to the graveyard. If one of them is a Trigger, the player may immediately cast/summon/use it without cost. Then all cards put into the graveyard are flipped face-down for indicating the received damage. The player loses the game at the moment the sixth card is turned face-down.

 

5. Precedence Rules :

  • •If a card's effects conflict with or modify the rules for the game, then the card effects take precedence. 

  • •Whenever two card effects of two different cards create a conflict with each other, then : 

    • ◦Time of activation, 

    • ◦Yes effects and 

    • ◦Turn order 

 take precedence respectively.

6. Abilities and Effects :

Abilities : Effect text preceded by keywords and/or other options.

Effects : Effect text not preceded by keywords and/or other options.

They have several categories :

Type-A or Immediate :

These effects have no duration and if the card with this ability/effect becomes destroyed while the ability/effect is resolving, the effect terminates.

Type-B or Durable :

These effects have a specific duration and if the card with this ability/effect becomes destroyed while the ability/effect is resolving, the effect continues resolving until the end of the duration.

Type-C or Activated :

These are effects that need a specific amount of energy as activation cost and has a predefined activation time and duration.

If the cost was paid, then the ability/effect will activate at  the required time and continues resolving until the end of the duration regardless of the existence of the character.

 

All abilities are optional, that is, their activation depends upon the decision of that card's owner. All effects are compulsory, meaning that they are activated whenever their condition of activation are met, without depending on the choice of the owning player unless stated otherwise by the card.

 

7. Inherent Energy abilities

Players use different kinds of energies for summoning, casting or using a card. These energies have different kinds of inherent abilities, or abilities that are granted automatically depending on the civility of the energy and the summoned character or cast sorcery, if an Environment of an appropriate type (“Field”) is present.

  • Basic energy : No abilities 

  • Flying energy : Characters powered (i.e. summoned with) by flying energy can only be attacked or blocked by characters powered  by flying energy. 

  • Aqua energy : While a Q environment card is in the the battlefield, characters powered by Q energy cannot be attacked or guarded by character not powered by Q energy. 

8. Shifting Rules

1. The battlefield is divided into columns equal to the highest no. of characters in a zone.

2. Each zone is a row.

3. At the pre-battle phase, characters may move to an adjacent column. They can not move more than one column per turn.

4. A character can attack directly the opponent if there are no characters in front of that character in that column.

5. Barrier Guards summoned for guarding such a character must be summon onto that character’s column.

9. Guidelines for Card Making

1 Total Energy : Power 1000 = 1:2 ~ 1:3 for cards with total energy ≤ 4

= 1:1 ~ 1:3

[Important : Card effects to be taken into account.]

2 Turnwheel :

        An card, whose card abilities and effects are strong enough for disrupting the game is called Turnwheel card. All abilities can be considered turnwheel below a specific cost (i.e. total energy of a card).

That amount of energy is called a turnwheel energy for that ability.

3 Card effects or abilities that directly damage any player's deck are forbidden.

4 Card effects or abilities that alter the number of cards in any player's energy zone are forbidden.

 

[Notes : 1 Characters can attack on the turn they entered the battle zone, but not the opponent until the start of the 5th turn.

2 Attacking is denoted by moving cards to the front zone/attack zone.]
