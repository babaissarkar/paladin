1. General Rules :

a. Card limit ― exactly 45 cards per library (standard game).
b. Copies of cards ―
        1. 2 copies of cards with total energy less than 6.
        2. 1 copy of card with cost more than or equal to 6.
        3. 1 additional copy of a card is allowed if that card has type Trap or has ability Trigger.

2. Parts of the Card :

a. Name
b. Required energy
        1. Generic Energy : It is denoted by a circled number, like this – ⑦. Generic energy required to use a card can be paid by any kind of energy. A ‘G’ placed before a number is equivalent to circling it in this case.
        2. Symbols : F, R and Q may be used in place of ↑, ↓ and ~ respectively. e.g, ↑5↓2 is equivalent to F5R2.
c. Civility
d. Civility type
e. Card type
f. Effect texts
g. Storyline
i. Energy no.
j. Additional symbols

3. Card Types :

a. Character
        1. Characters can attack on the turn they entered the battle zone, but not until the start of the 5th turn.
        2. Characters must attack an opponent's character in the same column of  battlefield before he/she can attack the player.
        3. Any character must attack an attackable character in the battle zone, if there was any, before that character can attack characters in the rest/rear zone.
        4. If a character has abilities or effects that require the player to to do something when that character attacks, then those  abilities or effects occur before attacking targets are selected.
        5. Engaged characters (characters in the attack zone) cannot attack again.
        6. If a character that has summon phase abilities/effects and becomes destroyed on entering the battle zone, its such abilities/effects will still trigger. (Due to the Precedence Rule, §6)
        7. Any character with power 0 or less is immediately destroyed.
        8. Any character with damage point 0 can still attack a character or an opponent, though that attack will not inflict any damage.
b. Sorcery
        1. Sorceries become destroyed after completely resolving.
        2. Attacker sorceries and Defender traps : When a player casts an attacker sorcery, the opponent may activate a trap in reply to the sorcery’s effect/ability. However, if after activation, it is found that the trap has the subtype ‘Defender’, the sorcery resolves as normally, but ceases to inflict any damage.
        3. Attacker sorceries can not damage opponent if his/her first effect can not be resolved.
c. Trap
        1. Traps become destroyed after completely resolving.
        2. Traps can be activated any time only during opponent's turn, after opponent has cast a sorcery or activated an effect or ability or declares an attack.
d. Environment
        1. If a player activates an Environment, any other environment(s) in the battle zone that are not of this environment's type is/are destroyed.
e. Armour
       1. An armour card is a type of card that can be summoned normally without any cost. The controller can use the attachment energy during a pre-battle phase to attach this armour with any of his/her characters. The armoured character then gains any bonuses and/or effects and/or abilities, as well as any of that armour’s efs./abls. that will activate after being armoured became activated as durable effects. These effects will stop resolving when the armour leaves the battle zone or their destruction criteria is met. When the armoured character leaves the battle zone, all armours armoured to it returns to their owner’s collection (hand). They do not require energy for summoning, but require energy for attaching.

4. Winning, Losing and Inflicting damage :

A player only wins the game when if his/her opponent's barrier points are reduced to zero. If a player was dealt more damage than he/she has barrier points, then the player who was dealt the damage loses the game. If any player has no choices to make during his/her turn, that player is also considered defeated and his opponent is declared winner (if there is only one opponent).
When an opponent's character attacks an opponent unguarded while the opponent has no guard by General, the opponent's barrier point is reduced by the attacking character's or sorcery's damage point.
When damage is being inflicted, the opponent immediately commences a special phase, known as damage phase.

5. Game-play :

NB. Any of the following phases may be skipped if the turn-player has nothing to do or chooses nothing to do.

a. beginning game phase :

        First, who takes the first turn is decided by a random method, like tossing a coin or rolling a dice. A player may also allow the opponent to start the game. Then each player takes six cards from the top of their library. These six cards are called the opening collection. If a player does not likes his/her collection, he/she may randomize (e.g. shuffle) those cards back to his/her deck and again take five cards again. This is called Collection refreshing. It can only be done at the beginning of the game and not more than twice. This phase is done only once, at the start of the game.

b. draw phase :

        In this phase, the current player (the player whose turn it is) adds a card to his/her collection. This is called drawing a card.

c. energy phase :

       In this phase, the player puts a card to the energy zone (known as energising) and disengages all cards in his/her energy zone. This phase is optional. A player can put only one card in the  energy zone per turn.

d. summoning phase :

I. Sub-phase 1 : using cards for energy :

        The current player uses (moves downward) the required no. of cards in his/her energy zone. The player can engage as many cards as he/she wants to, the only limit being the total number of cards in the energy zone and/or card effects. The total energy no. of cards thus used represents the currently active energy of that player, which is used for putting cards into the battlefield.

II. Sub-phase 2 : putting cards into the battlefield :

        The current player may now put any number of cards into the battlefield or materialize them into their specific zones into any half-empty or empty column (only if all other columns in the battle zone are full, i.e., is occupied by another of your characters). The only constraint being the active energy. The active energy decreases by the materialized card's total energy whenever a card is materialized. If a General is put into the battlefield, it should be put into the battlefield first. For characters, this process is called summoning and for sorceries, casting.

III. Sub-phase 3 : attacking and guarding :

Sub-phases :
A. Pre-battle phase,
B. Attack phase,
C. Post-battle phase.
A. Pre-battle phase :
I. The turn player selects which characters will attack this turn and moves them to the attack zone. This moving of cards from rest zone to attack zone is called engaging. Its reverse procedure is called disengaging. Then, the current player may shift one or more of his/her characters.
II. The turn player activates any effects that occur when a character attacks, from left to right.
B. Attack phase :
III. The turn player selects attack targets for those engaged characters.
IV. Battles occur between characters, if any.
C. Post-battle phase :
V. The total barrier damage is dealt to the opponent, if any and only if the source character which dealt the damage is present in the battle zone during this phase. This includes the damage from Attacker sorceries.

e. end phase

VI. The current turn player activates any abilities and effects that occur during the end of the turn. Then, the current player end his/her turn and the opponent plays his/her turn by taking phases b to e. The games continues until the criteria as stated in rule 3 is met.

f. *damage phase

VII. This is a special phase that is commenced when a player receives damage from the opponent. In this phase, the player puts cards from the top of the deck to the graveyard. If one of them is a Trigger, the player may immediately cast/summon/use it without cost. Then all cards put into the graveyard are flipped face-down for indicating the received damage. The player loses the game at the moment the sixth card is turned face-down.

6. Precedence Rules :

1. If a card's effects conflict with or modify the rules for the game, then the card effects take precedence.
2. Whenever two card effects of two different cards create a conflict with each other, then :
  • Time of activation,
  • Yes effects and
  • Turn order
take precedence respectively from top to bottom order.

7. Abilities and Effects :

1. Definitions :

1. Abilities : Effect text preceded by keywords and/or other options.
2. Effects : Effect text not preceded by keywords and/or other options.

2. Categories :

They have several categories :
Type-A or Immediate :
These effects have no duration and if the card with this ability/effect becomes destroyed while the ability/effect is resolving, the effect terminates.
Type-B or Durable :
These effects have a specific duration and if the card with this ability/effect becomes destroyed while the ability/effect is resolving, the effect continues resolving until the end of the duration.
Type-C or Activated :
These are effects that need a specific amount of energy as activation cost and has a predefined activation time and duration.
If the cost was paid, then the ability/effect will activate at the required time and continues resolving until the end of the duration regardless of the existence of the character.
All abilities are optional, that is, their activation depends upon the decision of that card's owner. All effects are compulsory, meaning that they must be activated whenever their condition of activation are met, without depending on the choice of the owning player unless stated otherwise by the card.
4. If ability/effect allows a player to do more than one action in the game, then that ability/effect is qualified as “Compound”. Each action is considered a part of that ability/effect.
5. If a sorcery/trap 's first effect/first part of a compound about can not be resolved, then that sorcery can not be cast or that trap can not be activated. Though that sorcery can be activated by its Trigger ability.

The Barrier Guard character is reshuffled to the deck at the end of the turn it is summoned.

4. Field :
Players use different kinds of energies for summoning, casting or using a card. These energies have different kinds of inherent abilities, or abilities that are granted automatically depending on the civility of the energy and the summoned character or cast sorcery, if an Environment of an appropriate type (“Field”) is present.
Basic energy : No abilities
Flying energy : Characters powered (i.e. summoned with) by flying energy can only be attacked or guarded by characters powered  by flying energy.
Aqua energy : While a Q environment card is in the the battlefield, characters powered by Q energy cannot be attacked or guarded by character not powered by Q energy.
5. Fury Blade # :
When a character with Fury Blade battles, decrease the power of the battling character by # during the battle.
Fury blade activates when the character with Fury blade battles.
6. Heal # : If you are damaged since the start of the game, increase your barrier points by #.
7. Hidden Blade # : If a character with Hidden Blade # attacks the opponent directly and is guarded, then if the character with Hidden Blade wins the battle (the battle here means the battle due to Barrier Guard ability),  then the opponent receives # damage.

9. Columns :
	1. The number of columns in the battle zone is equal to the number of characters of the player with more characters, or equal to the number of characters in any side if the both players have equal number of characters. A column can only contain two characters, one from each player.
	(*) Empty column or null column : a column without any character. 
	(*) Half-empty column : a column occupied by one of your or your opponent’s characters.
	(*) Full column : a column with two characters, one from each player.
	2. A character can only attack a opponents’ character that resides in the same column. 
	3. A character must be put onto a column if there exists one. Otherwise, a null column is created and the character is put onto that column. The summoning player may put the character that is to be summoned into any pre-existing empty or half-empty column.
	4. Shifting : A character can move to an adjacent empty column during that character’s controller’s pre-battle phase.
	5. Collapse rule : If such a situation occurs, when two characters controlled by different players reside in separate adjacent columns and those columns have only one character in each column (i.e., if those are half-empty columns), then these columns are immediately combined to form a single column with those two characters.

10. Guidelines for Card Making

1. Total Energy : (Power / 1000) = 1:2 ~ 1:3 for cards with total energy ≤ 4
= 1:1 ~ 1:3
[Important : Card effects to be taken into account.]
2. Turnwheel :
An card, whose card abilities and effects are strong enough for changing the outcome of the game against the player with advantage  is called a Turnwheel card. All abilities can be considered turnwheel below a specific cost (i.e. total energy of a card). That amount of energy is called  turnwheel energy for that ability.
3. Card effects or abilities that directly damage any player's deck are forbidden.
4. Card effects or abilities that alter the number of cards in any player's energy zone are forbidden.
5. Any effect/ability of a sorcery, trap or character cannot target an environment.
6. If a card requires only one kind of energy for using, that card's total energy must not exceed 5. If a card requires more than one kind of energy, the value of each type of energy required must not be more than 7.
e.g., 	↑6 and ↑5↓2 are legal, but  ↑8 and ↑8↓1 is illegal.
This rule does not applies to Generic energy.
7. A card’s energy no. type is decided by its energy. First, the type of which the card requires the maximum energy is found. The card must have one energy no. of this type. Next, it is checked if any other energy type requires a number of energy that is within 2 points of the energy with maximum no. of energies. If there exists such a type, the card must also have a energy no. of that type. All other types have no influence on the energy no. of the card.
Examples :
A card with ↑6↓2 energy has energy no. ↑1↓0, while a card with energy ↑6↓2~5 has energy ↑1~1.
Note : Cards of the Niaz civility always have energy no. ↑1~1.
